Software Development

Preamble

I could write a novel about the causes and effects in my life that moved me towards a career in software development. However, I can name three formative experiences/things that form a loose outline:

  1. Building and programming multiple Lego Mindstorms robots in elementary school;
  2. Participating on an FRC team (5690 SubZero Robotics);
  3. My father's love for asking questions, figuring out math, and reading Wikipedia.

Projects

  • Drone simulation built with C++ for web deployment
  • Human/Robot interaction, vision research with UMN's IRVLab
  • TransitPal, a web tool for tracking public transit usage

Where I'm (planning on) going

Aside from Scotland, home of my ancestors I am chiefly interested in lower level development, whether that be embedded systems or OS/kernel development. Years of living with engineers and robots has drilled into me a certain love for the physical applications of the digital world.

All Hail C!

I really love programming in C; it gives me the high of feeling like I'm in control, while also being miles less painful than assembly. It's also mostly non-paradigmatic, not quite Object Oriented but certainly not functional. Instead, it provides an abstract interface to your machine's resources that is unbiased and transparent.

Obviously, C isn't suited to all computing tasks; for everyday problem solving, I'm likely to default to Python (another favorite of mine). But Python can never scratch that certain need-to-know-what-and-why itch of mine like C can.
